docs: JIT/numba feasibility study + finding (REJECT/DEFER)

Empirical study of three "go faster on the remaining IIR hot paths"
options after the v0.3.0 vectorisation:

* numba: REJECT — no win-arm64 wheel (dev box), +39 MB bundle,
  needs AOT pipeline for frozen .exe, and the per-bar Python loop
  win is already neutralised by the vectorised baseline.
* Cython: REJECT — five-target wheel-build matrix is
  disproportionate to the diminishing-return gain.
* scipy.signal.lfilter: DEFER — 12x speedup on the bare iir_tail
  kernel (bit-equivalent to 2.75e-14), projected ~7x on SMI and
  ~2x on LRSI compute_arr. Wins are absolute-small (12 ms on a
  25-symbol scan) and dwarfed by the +23-35 MB bundle inflation.
  Revisit if the user adopts large-universe scans where SMI/LRSI
  dominate, or if a future indicator with >50ms compute lands.

Zero-dep alternatives still on the table (allocation reduction in
iir_tail, LRSI 4-stage Laguerre fusion) — both pure-numpy
refactors that would close ~2-3x more without any new dep. Parked
behind the perf-gate.

docs/JIT_FEASIBILITY.md captures the full wheel-availability
matrix, bundle-impact numbers, microbenchmarks, projected wins,
and the explicit re-evaluation triggers. docs/PERFORMANCE.md
"Future work" updated to point at the finding.

Closes the numba-feasibility todo.
